There were large variations in the effectiveness and results of change and improvement activities due to contingent factors of organizations. In this research, success factors and process levels of SPI(Software Process Improvement) in Korean software industry were empirically investigated with the two contingent variables ? organization size and SPI roles. Large organizations were significantly better in the achievement of important SPI success factors and key process areas of CMM level 2 and 3. The relative importance of success factors was significantly different among different SPI roles. There were different perceptions on SPI success factors and process levels between the SPI planning and supporting group, top managers and SEPG personnel, and the SPI implementing group, project managers and development staffs.
