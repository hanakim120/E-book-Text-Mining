It is important to have capabilities of developing and maintaining highly reliable software products within estimated budgets and schedules. An effective method to obtain these goals is the process improvement through objective assessments of software processes. ISO9000, CMM, SPICE are international standards which have been developed for this purpose. In the study, characteristics of these models are summarized, and differences and development histories are analyzed comparatively. With this information, an organization unit can establish an efficient software quality management system by selecting the best suitable assessment model, and reducing redundant assessments and reports.
